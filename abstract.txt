Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) has emerged as a transformative approach that combines information retrieval with large language models. While RAG demonstrates considerable promise for knowledge-intensive tasks, its application in IT service desk operations remains fragmented and relatively underexplored. This systematic mapping study offers the first comprehensive analysis of RAG research within IT service desk contexts. Following established SMS protocols, we conducted a systematic literature search across five academic databases using a three-tier search strategy. From an initial pool of 764 papers, we applied dual-dimension relevance screening (RAG relevance ≥3 AND Helpdesk relevance ≥2 on 1-5 scales) followed by full-text quality assessment. This process resulted in 71 methodologically rigorous papers selected for detailed analysis. Our analysis reveals explosive recent growth, with 69% of publications concentrated in the 2024-2025 period. We identified 20 unique RAG techniques, dominated by dense retrieval (41 papers) and BM25 (31 papers), and documented 24 distinct IT service desk applications. These applications are led by knowledge management (21 papers) and incident management (18 papers). Quality assessment demonstrates robust reliability, showing 87% average overall confidence and 96% of papers classified as high confidence. We further identified 28 implementation challenges, with data quality (22 papers) and performance (18 papers) emerging as primary concerns. The dramatic shift from technical focus (11% of papers) to practical ITSM applications (89% with high/medium ITSM specificity) indicates field maturation toward operational deployment. This study provides the first comprehensive systematic mapping with quantitative evidence, establishing both a baseline and research agenda for advancing RAG adoption in service desk environments.
