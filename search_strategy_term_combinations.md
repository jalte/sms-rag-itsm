# Three-Tier Search Strategy: Complete Term Combinations

## Document Purpose

This document provides a comprehensive explanation of the three-tier search strategy employed in the systematic mapping study on "Retrieval-Augmented Generation in IT Service Desk Operations." The strategy utilized 25 term combinations across five academic databases (Scopus, ACM Digital Library, IEEE Xplore, ScienceDirect, and Web of Science) plus backward snowballing.

## Search Strategy Overview

**Search Execution Period:** August 2025
**Databases Searched:** 5 primary databases + secondary search (backward snowballing)
**Total Initial Results:** 764 unique papers (658 primary + 120 secondary - 14 duplicates)
**Timeframe Filter:** 2020-2025 (capturing RAG emergence and evolution)
**Language Filter:** English only

## Search Strategy Rationale

### Why Three Tiers?

1. **Tier 1 (High Priority):** Focuses on direct RAG-service desk combinations with highest expected relevance. These combinations target papers explicitly discussing RAG implementations in service desk contexts, maximizing precision.

2. **Tier 2 (Medium Priority):** Expands to specific ITSM processes (incident management, ticket resolution) and broader support contexts (technical support). Balances precision with increased recall.

3. **Tier 3 (Comprehensive):** Ensures no relevant papers are missed by covering all combinations of RAG terminology variants, ITSM process terms, and action/context terms. Prioritizes recall over precision.

### Why 25 Combinations?

The 25 combinations represent systematic coverage of:
- **3 RAG term variants** ×
- **8 service desk/ITSM domain terms** ×
- **5 action/context terms** =
- **120 potential combinations** (reduced to 25 high-value combinations through strategic selection)

The selection strategy prioritized:
- **High-frequency term pairs** (e.g., RAG + helpdesk)
- **Domain-specific terminology** (e.g., ITSM, incident management)
- **Implementation-focused terms** (e.g., implementation, framework, deployment)
- **Avoiding redundancy** while maintaining comprehensive coverage

### Database-Specific Adaptations

Different databases required syntax adaptations while maintaining semantic equivalence:

#### Scopus
```
TITLE-ABS-KEY(RAG AND helpdesk AND implementation)
```

#### ACM Digital Library
```
[[All: RAG] AND [All: helpdesk] AND [All: implementation]]
```

#### IEEE Xplore
```
("RAG" AND "helpdesk" AND "implementation")
```

#### ScienceDirect
```
RAG AND helpdesk AND implementation
```

#### Web of Science
```
TS=(RAG AND helpdesk AND implementation)
```

## Quality Assurance Measures

### Duplicate Detection
- **Cross-database duplicates:** 11 relevant papers identified and removed
- **Secondary search duplicates:** 8 papers identified and removed
- **Total duplicates removed:** 19 papers
- **Deduplication rate:** 2.4% (19/783 before deduplication)

### Relevance Filtering
- **Dual-dimension scoring:** RAG relevance (1-5) AND Helpdesk relevance (1-5)
- **Optimal threshold:** RAG ≥3 AND Helpdesk ≥2
- **Sensitivity analysis:** Three threshold configurations evaluated
- **Final selection:** 71 papers meeting quality criteria (confidence ≥75% AND high/medium ITSM specificity)

### Coverage Validation
- **Database diversity:** All 5 major databases covered
- **Temporal coverage:** 2020-2025 (69.0% from 2024-2025)
- **ITSM specificity:** 85.9% high specificity, 88.7% high/medium combined
- **Confidence quality:** 87.0% average, 95.8% high confidence (>75%)

## Search Strategy Strengths

1. **Systematic Coverage:** All major RAG terminology variants covered
2. **Domain Specificity:** ITSM-specific terminology ensuring relevant results
3. **Balanced Approach:** Precision (Tier 1) balanced with recall (Tier 3)
4. **Database Complementarity:** Different databases captured different paper types
5. **Secondary Search Integration:** Backward snowballing added 29.6% of final papers
6. **Quality Over Quantity:** Strict filtering ensured high-quality final dataset

## Search Strategy Limitations

1. **Language Bias:** English-only papers (estimated >90% coverage in computer science)
2. **Database Selection:** Five databases may miss domain-specific venues (mitigated by snowballing)
3. **Temporal Snapshot:** August 2025 cutoff in rapidly evolving field
4. **Grey Literature:** Excluded non-peer-reviewed content (blog posts, whitepapers)
5. **Terminology Evolution:** RAG terminology still evolving; some variants may be missed
